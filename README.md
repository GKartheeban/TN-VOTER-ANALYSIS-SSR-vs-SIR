This comprehensive analysis evaluates the integrity and evolution of Tamil Nadu’s electoral data by comparing Special Summary Revision (SSR) and Special Intensive Revision (SIR) datasets. Developed using Excel and Power BI, the project transforms raw voter rolls into actionable insights regarding demographic shifts and administrative accuracy.

Project Overview
The core objective is to measure the "purification" of voter lists. While SSR is a routine update based on individual claims, SIR involves exhaustive door-to-door verification. This project quantifies the impact of these two distinct methodologies on data quality and voter representation across Tamil Nadu's constituencies.

Technical Workflow
Data Engineering (Excel):

Cleaned and standardized raw electoral data from multiple districts.

Performed deduplication and categorized entries into Additions, Deletions, and Corrections.

Calculated percentage variances between SSR and SIR cycles to identify outliers.

Visual Analytics (Power BI):

Geospatial Analysis: Used map visuals to highlight districts with the highest deletion rates, indicating areas previously prone to "ghost" voters.

Demographic Slicing: Created DAX measures to track gender ratios and the inclusion of first-time voters (18–19 age bracket).

Trend Comparison: Developed side-by-side bar charts to show how SIR significantly "thinned" bloated rolls compared to the incremental updates of SSR.

Key Research Objectives
Identify Inflation: Detect constituencies where the voter-to-population ratio exceeded logical thresholds during SSR.

Verify SIR Efficacy: Measure the net reduction in ineligible voters (Absent, Shifted, or Deceased) following intensive ground-level verification.

Trend Forecasting: Provide a data-backed view of voter turnout potential based on the "cleaner" SIR lists.

Impact
This analysis serves as a vital tool for election observers and administrators, proving that intensive, ground-level audits (SIR) are essential for maintaining democratic transparency and preventing electoral malpractice.
